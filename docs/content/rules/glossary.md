Definitions of terms used throughout the Olympia rulebook. Each entry links to
the section where the term is described in full.

## A

**Adept**
: The third [experience](../skills-magic/) level for a skill, reached at 12–20 successful uses.

**Admit**
: A permission, set with the `ADMIT` order, that lets units from other factions
  stack with a unit or enter a building or ship it controls. The default refuses
  entry to other factions. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Ally**
: The qualification shown in a combat report for a unit that joined a battle
  because of a `DEFEND` declaration rather than its own attack. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Apprentice**
: The lowest [experience](../skills-magic/) level for a skill, covering 0–4 successful uses.

**Attitude**
: A unit's declared combat stance toward another unit or faction: `HOSTILE`,
  `DEFEND`, `NEUTRAL`, or `DEFAULT`. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Aura**
: The mystical force a mage must possess to cast spells. Characters have a current
  and a maximum aura level; current aura is spent casting and replenishes two points
  per turn. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

## B

**Beast**
: A creature counted for the Beastmastery skills, such as a dragon or lion. Beasts
  may serve as fighters but cannot learn skills or hold items. See [Reference Tables](../tables/).

**Behind**
: A unit's declared battle position, set with the `BEHIND` order, placing it in the
  front or rear. Rear units are targeted only after the front is killed. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Body**
: The item a noble becomes upon death, recoverable with `EXPLORE`. A body decomposes
  one and one half game years after death. See [Health & Death](../health-death/).

**Break point**
: The threshold at which a side loses a battle: when its combined offensive and
  defensive value falls by 50%. See [Combat](../combat/).

## C

**Castle**
: A building that anchors land ownership, collecting taxes from its province and
  from garrisons bound to it. One castle is allowed per province. See
  [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/) and [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/).

**Category skill**
: A top-level skill (e.g. Shipcraft [600]) that must be learned before any of its
  sub-skills. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Catch-up NP**
: Extra starting [Noble Points](#n) granted to players who join the game late, so
  all players hold roughly equal Noble Points. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Character**
: See [Noble](#n). Used interchangeably with "noble."

**Civilization level**
: A rating of a province's development, set by its cities and buildings or half the
  maximum of its neighbors, whichever is higher. Level zero is wilderness. It
  determines the [tax base](#t). See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Cloudlands**
: A small region floating over Mt. Olympus, reachable mainly by flight, home to the
  cities Nimbus, Stratos, and Aerovia. See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Conjurer**
: The lowest [magician status](#m) label, for a maximum aura of 6–10. See
  [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Contract**
: A [loyalty](#l) bond based on gold invested in a noble. It decays each month and
  leaves the noble open to bribery. See [Loyalty, Stacking & Upkeep](../logistics/).

## D

**Decree**
: An order issued by a province ruler instructing all garrisons to watch for or
  attack a given unit. See [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/).

**Default**
: The combat [attitude](#a) of any unit not on a `HOSTILE`, `DEFEND`, or `NEUTRAL`
  list: neutral to other factions, defending units of its own faction. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Defend**
: The combat [attitude](#a) of aiding another unit if it is attacked. See [Combat](../combat/).

## E

**Effort**
: The work, measured in worker-days, required to complete a building or ship. See
  [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/) and [Ships](../ships/).

**Entity**
: Anything in the Olympian world with a unique [entity number](#e) — a noble, item,
  location, or skill. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Entity number**
: The unique code shown in brackets after a name, e.g. `[5499]`. For a province it
  also encodes [map coordinates](#m). See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Experience**
: The count of turns in which a skill was used successfully, mapping to levels from
  apprentice to grand master. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

## F

**Faction**
: All the units controlled by one player. A player begins with one character and may
  grow the faction by hiring or persuading others. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Faery**
: A separate reality reachable through faery hills, guarded by the Faery Hunt. See
  [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Fast study**
: Points, granted to new players, each of which substitutes for a day of `STUDY`
  without counting toward the study limit. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Fear**
: A [loyalty](#l) bond based on the severity of `TERRORIZE` used on a noble. It
  decays each month and leaves the noble open to bribery. See
  [Loyalty, Stacking & Upkeep](../logistics/).

**Fee**
: The charge a ferry captain sets, in gold per 100 weight of a passenger's stack,
  for use of the `BOARD` order. See [Ships](../ships/).

**Ferry**
: A ship operated commercially to carry paying passengers, run with the `FEE`,
  `BOARD`, `FERRY`, and `UNLOAD` orders. See [Ships](../ships/).

**Fighter**
: A trained man who fights in battle — soldier, pikeman, swordsman, knight, elite
  guard, crossbowman, archer, or elite archer. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Fortification**
: A structure's defensive bonus, added to the defense of the men it shelters during
  battle. See [Combat](../combat/).

## G

**Galley**
: A rowed warship requiring 14 sailors and carrying up to 5,000 units of cargo. See [Ships](../ships/).

**Garrison**
: A group of at least ten soldiers stationed in a province to claim it, guard against
  pillaging, and collect taxes for a castle. See [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/).

**GM**
: The game master, who moderates the game, publishes notices in the [Olympia Times](#o),
  and rules on bugs and compensation. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Gold**
: The Olympian currency, item [1]. It weighs nothing and does not accumulate when
  left in a province at turn's end. See [Markets](../markets/).

**Grand master**
: The highest [experience](../skills-magic/) level for a skill, reached at 35+ uses.

## H

**Hades**
: A subterranean world, also called the Land of the Dead, populated by demons,
  ghouls, and spirits. See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Health**
: A noble's rating from 1–100 indicating how wounded it is; zero means death. Only
  nobles have health. See [Health & Death](../health-death/).

**Hidden route**
: A route revealed by `EXPLORE`, usable only by factions whose units have crossed
  it. See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Hostile**
: The combat [attitude](#a) of attacking another unit on sight. See [Combat](../combat/).

## I

**Illness**
: A condition, flagged on a noble, that drains health each week until cured or
  fought off. See [Health & Death](../health-death/).

**Inn**
: A building that earns monthly income and raises a sheltered noble's weekly chance
  of fighting off illness. See [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/).

**Inner location**
: A sub-location within a province, such as a city, inn, or cave, normally entered
  only from the surrounding province. See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Item**
: Anything a character may possess — gold, weapons, scrolls, potions, lumber, and
  more. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

## J

**Journeyman**
: The second [experience](../skills-magic/) level for a skill, covering 5–11 successful uses.

## L

**Lore sheet**
: The description delivered when a skill is learned, listing background and the
  sub-skills studyable directly under it. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Loyalty**
: The bond tying a noble to its lord, of one kind at a time: [contract](#c),
  [oath](#o), or [fear](#f). See [Loyalty, Stacking & Upkeep](../logistics/).

## M

**Magician status**
: A label given by maximum aura — conjurer, mage, wizard, or sorcerer. See
  [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Maintenance cost**
: The gold a noble must pay its men each month, or lose them to departure or
  starvation. See [Loyalty, Stacking & Upkeep](../logistics/).

**Map coordinates**
: A province's position, read from its [entity number](#e): the leading letters give
  the row and the trailing number the column, with `[a1]` at the northwest corner. See
  [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Market**
: The matching of `BUY` and `SELL` orders, which occurs only when buyer and seller
  are in the same city. See [Markets](../markets/).

**Men**
: Non-descript followers held as possessions — peasants, workers, sailors, soldiers,
  and the like. Men cannot learn skills, hold items, or act independently. See
  [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Mine**
: A building, allowed once per mountain province or rocky hill, that extracts iron,
  gold, and rarer elements as it deepens. See [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/).

**Month**
: One game turn, lasting 30 game days. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

## N

**Noble**
: An individual under a player's control, also called a character. Nobles give all
  player orders and may hold items, learn skills, fight, and cast spells. See
  [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Noble Points (NP)**
: A currency for buying nobles with `FORM`, learning advanced skills, and securing
  oath loyalty. Players gain one every eighth turn. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

## O

**Oath**
: A non-decaying [loyalty](#l) bond secured with one or two Noble Points, yielding
  oath-1 or oath-2. See [Loyalty, Stacking & Upkeep](../logistics/).

**Olympia Times**
: The newspaper published with each turn, carrying GM notices, signed press, and
  rumors. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Opium**
: A good produced in swamps and demanded by other markets; selling it raises future
  demand but lowers a city's tax base. See [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/).

**Order**
: A command queued for a unit. Untrusted order files are the validation boundary of
  the engine. See [Orders](../orders/).

**Order scanner**
: The service that parses incoming order files and replies with any errors. See [Orders](../orders/).

**Order template**
: The listing of all a player's units and their pending orders, printed at the end of
  a turn report and reusable as the next order set. See [Orders](../orders/).

## P

**Partially known skill**
: A skill discovered through `RESEARCH` that must still be studied before it can be
  used. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Pillage**
: Seizing a location's tax base with ten or more fighters via `PILLAGE`, which lowers
  its future revenue. See [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/).

**Player character (PC)**
: The character a player starts with, beginning at oath-2 loyalty. Nothing is special
  about it beyond being first. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Player entity**
: The placeholder entity representing a faction. It is invisible, takes few orders,
  and issues none of a character's usual commands. See [Orders](../orders/).

**Pledge**
: Swearing one's lands to another noble with `PLEDGE`, granting that noble status
  while income still flows to the castles. See [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/).

**Port city**
: A coastal city founded as an ocean port; ships reach the ocean only through it, not
  the surrounding province. See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Prayer**
: A sub-skill of Religion [750]. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Press**
: Signed items submitted to the [Olympia Times](#o) with the `PRESS` order. See
  [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Priest**
: A character who has learned Religion [750]. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Prisoner**
: A character captured in battle or by `SURRENDER`. Prisoners report nothing, execute
  no orders, and may escape. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Province**
: A location on the map grid, of a single terrain type, possibly containing
  sub-locations. See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

## R

**Rank**
: A noble's title — lord, knight, baron, count, earl, marquess, duke, or king — set
  by the number of provinces controlled. See [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/).

**Region**
: A named collection of provinces forming a continent, island, or ocean. See
  [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Relic**
: A unique artifact introduced through quests, such as the Imperial Throne or Crown
  of Prosperity. Most return to the netherworld after use. See [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/).

**Research**
: Discovering hidden sub-skills, mostly spells, performed in a tower (or temple for
  Religion) by its owner. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Roundship**
: A wide merchant sailing ship requiring eight sailors and carrying up to 25,000
  units of cargo. See [Ships](../ships/).

**Ruler**
: A noble who controls a province, directly through a castle or indirectly through
  pledges. A province may have several rulers in a pledge chain. See
  [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/).

**Rumor**
: Unsigned items submitted to the [Olympia Times](#o) with the `RUMOR` order. See
  [Playing the Game](../playing/).

## S

**Safe haven**
: A starting city in which combat and magic are forbidden, where new players begin.
  See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**School of magic**
: One of the six magical category skills: Magic, Weather magic, Scrying, Gatecraft,
  Artifact construction, and Necromancy. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Siege engine**
: A device — catapult, battering ram, or siege tower — that always targets a
  structure in battle and is not used at sea. See [Combat](../combat/).

**Skill**
: Knowledge a character may learn to perform tasks, organized into category skills
  and sub-skills. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Spell**
: A sub-skill of a [school of magic](#s), cast using knowledge, required items, and
  aura. See [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Stack**
: A group of units joined so they move and fight together. See
  [Loyalty, Stacking & Upkeep](../logistics/).

**Stack leader**
: The top-most unit in a stack, the only one that may initiate combat and the last to
  take hits in battle. See [Loyalty, Stacking & Upkeep](../logistics/) and [Combat](../combat/).

**Status**
: See [Rank](#r).

**Sub-location**
: See [Inner location](#i).

**Sub-skill**
: A skill within a category, learnable only after its category skill. See
  [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

## T

**Tax base**
: The gold a province or city generates each month, set by its civilization level. It
  may be taxed by a castle or garrison, or seized by pillaging, and does not
  accumulate. See [Provinces & Relics](../provinces/).

**Temple**
: A building in which Religion is studied and researched, yielding monthly offerings
  to a priest owner. See [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/) and
  [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Tower**
: A building used as a laboratory for `RESEARCH` by its owner. Up to six may be built
  inside a castle. See [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/) and
  [Skills, Magic & Religion](../skills-magic/).

**Tradegood**
: A good found for sale or in demand through the Trade [730] skills, traded for
  profit between city markets. See [Markets](../markets/).

**Turn**
: One processing cycle of the game, covering one game month. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

**Turn report**
: The per-player account of what happened during a turn, ending with the order
  template. See [Playing the Game](../playing/).

## U

**Unit**
: Any entity that takes orders or appears in a location — most often a noble. See
  [Playing the Game](../playing/).

## W

**Wilderness**
: A province with a civilization level of zero. See [Geography & Movement](../geography/).

**Worker-days**
: The unit of [effort](#e) for construction: one worker laboring for one day. See
  [Buildings & Economy](../buildings-economy/).

**Wound**
: Damage reducing a noble's health, randomly 1–100 in combat, which may cause illness
  or death. See [Health & Death](../health-death/).
